CV

My research interest focuses on computational mental health. Specifically, I strive to:

  • investigate and identify potentially harmful behaviors on social media to promote healthy online communication
  • develop interactive intervention and health tracking system supported by communication technology to maximize emotional functioning and build long-term empathy
My ultimate goal is to research and develop a new form of therapeutic technique that combines digital interventions (e.g. social media, mobile app, VR/AR) with traditional approaches.

Project Overview

Project in Progress - Updates Coming Soon
VR and Pet Loss Syndrome: this project aims to develop an interactive online experience to help people cope with sudden and unexpected pet loss (e.g. sending a virtual letter to your dog), and examine the psychological effects and essential postexperience support system of digital grief therapy.
Anti-Bullying Twitter Bot: this study aims to identify and flag abusive behaviors on Twitter, and assesses the duration of online behavior change. The primary goal of this research is to examine the factors that contribute to long-lasting online behavior change.

Digital Conversation Keeper for Caregivers and Asylum Seekers
Collaborators: Center for Transcultural Psychiatry, FPC de Oostvaarderskliniek
[Project in Progress] We aim to design a culturally sensitive 'online interactive environment' that capture the understanding of the patients on their treatment trajectory. Our goal is to bridge the gap between the care provided by the Center for Transcultural Psychiatry and patients with complex psychiatric problems.

Modeling Health Messages on Instagram
Collaborators: Hunter Priniski, Keith Holyoak
[Project in Progress] This research assesses and models health information on Instagram, and discusses how the resulting model can be leveraged to build a scalable intervention that reduces mental health risks of some health influencers' posting behaviors.

Mobile UX Research on Mental Health Application
[Project in Progress] While TalkLife, an online peer to peer support network for mental health, has been systematically developed by professionals, this project conducts usability testing, eye movement tracking, computational analysis on survey data, and final prototype testing to further understand user needs and behaviors. The primary goal of this study is to investigate and minimize the potential side effects of online support network by examining diverse definitions of supportive online community.

Subreddit Analysis: Retrieval of Traumatic Memories
[Project in Progress] Using computational text methods (LDA, TF-IDF, SVM, CNN, etc) supplemented by qualitative analysis, this study strives to identify potential factors that are correlated with retrieval of traumatic memories from a large-scale Subreddit data. Ideally, this research will shed light on important elements that need to be carefully examined during my following study on graduated VR Exposure Therapy.

Impact of Interactive AI Technology on Product Perception
This projects aims to investigate users’ expectations and feedback on AI personal assistants and examine the factors that influence user preferences. According to the findings, the device compatibility and functions outweigh the brand popularity in consumer decision, and the product perception is shaped by social context, media form, marketing strategy and product cost.

Twitter Network Analysis on Trump Impeachment
This study identifies eight communities that sparked discussion on Trump impeachment from eigenvector centrality scores. While this project focuses on computational identification of influential factors using network analysis and sentiment calculation, qualitative study on individual tweets is necessary to complement further investigation.
