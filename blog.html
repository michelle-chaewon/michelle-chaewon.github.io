Awards & Achievements


Global Goals Jam 2020
I participated in the Global Goals Jam where creative individuals around the world bring the world a step closer to address long-term social challenges. Our team consulted with external experts to discuss future research directions for developing mental health digital intervention.


Distinguished smile UP Ambassador
I was selected as a distinguished ambassador at smile UP (learning application). My primary duty was to promote active discussions on diverse topics like digital mental health, and develop project ideas on communication technology that can help child victims of domestic violence during coronavirus (upcoming project).

Distinguished Brand Ambassador
I was selected as a distinguished brand ambassador at Samsung during the Paralympics. I was primarily responsible for introducing various functional benefits of Samsung VR and assisting international customers with disabilities to fully participate in VR experience.

2018 Kimbo Scholarship
I was awarded $2000 scholarship from Kimbo Foundation. According to the website, the mission of the Kimbo Foundation is to recognize and encourage students who show exceptional academic achievement and leadership potential for the Korean community, and to provide them with financial support in their endeavour to study.

UCLA Computational Sociology Working Group
The aim of the Computational Sociology Working Group (CompSoc) is to discover and practice the latest methods in computational sociology. Our activities include: programming, software and data dessions, workshops for students' projects and fellowship applications, guest lectures, and a journal club.

Amnesty International is a global movement of more than 7 million people who are campaigning for human rights protection. Amnesty International has been helping people claim their rights and speaking out against injustice worldwide.

UCLA CALPIRG Students
Our goal is to strengthen our open educational resource (OER) programs at the UCs and ensure they are well-used, as well as reduce or eliminate the use of access codes. We are working to show student support, get faculty to pledge to give preference to cheaper course materials, and work with administrators to institutionalize OER.
